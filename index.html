The Death Of Stealth

Quantum magnetometers can detect submarines by their magnetic signature. Quantum gravimeters can map underground tunnels. The innovation isn't just better sensors—it's that the fundamental advantage of stealth (hiding from classical detection) becomes obsolete. You can't hide mass, magnetism, or gravitational effects. Quantum sensing doesn't look for signatures you emit; it measures distortions in fields you can't help but create just by existing.
